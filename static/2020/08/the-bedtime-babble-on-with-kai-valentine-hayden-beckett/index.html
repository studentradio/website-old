The Bedtime Babble On, with Kai Valentine & Hayden Beckett

 5th August 2020 |  North East, Station Focus

[Kai]

At the start of June, we sat in a meeting with lecturers at the University of Sunderland about our plans for our major dissertation project for the Radio MA. 

 

We had no idea what we were going to do. 

 

Any plans we had in place, in tatters or in the very least tainted by the COVID-19 crisis & the national lockdown in place. With no access to the state of the art studios of Spark & no matter how much support the fantastic lecturers gave us, we were worried that whatever we were able to muster up in our living rooms wouldn’t be to the standard of ‘what could have been’… but we’ve realised over the past few months, the industry can adapt, and we’ve learnt techniques now that will change the way we make radio for years to come.

 

A bit of background on us, Hayden & I started working together back in October as presenter & producer of the Friday Breakfast show. When the world turned upside down in March, the Spark management team, made up of mainly MA Radio students & other committed student volunteers, decided that we needed to continue to broadcast from home to abide by our OFCOM guidelines, and set about making it possible. We streamlined our schedule & active volunteer base due to the unknowing nature of what was to come & a modest but hard-working group of student volunteers took on extra programmes & responsibilities, with both live & voice tracked programming to keep the station running & supporting the student & wider community throughout the crisis.

 

Five months on, the station is still running strong & we are now in a position to welcome back more volunteers into the fold, creating and actively working on programmes again. We may not have access to our studios quite yet, but we have learnt to adapt & overcome the obstacles involved with broadcasting from home.

 

So, where did the Bedtime Babble-On begin? As mentioned, Hayden & I had no idea what we wanted to do now for our dissertation projects, but what we did know is that we’d both found a new radio passion, late-night talk radio. As someone who is living alone through the pandemic & a Metro journey away from friends, I spent the first 94 days of lockdown without any face to face human interaction. I was quick to realise that the loneliest times were late into the evenings. Early on, I started listening into The Late Night Alternative with Iain Lee & Katherine Boyle & I fell in love with this incredible little world I’d stumbled across. Hayden & I would chat the next day about calls we’d heard & how we’d love to do a show like that one day. 

 

Then, as you’ll probably all know, Iain & Katherine’s show came to an end & I spent the next few weeks looking around for (ironically) an alternative. We quickly realised that there aren’t many non-political late-night call-in radio shows out there, somewhere for the callers to just chat about their day, what’s on their mind and to be a friend in the lonely hours. With so many cutbacks taking place across the industry, these shows, that aren’t exactly money-makers, are understandably, but sadly, the first for the chop. 

 

So, we thought, why don’t we have a go? Surely if I’m feeling lonely on a night & missing the company, others out there will be too?

 

So, for our dissertation project, Hayden & I teamed up to present & produce a 3-hour, late-night call-in show, 4 nights a week, throughout July. The Bedtime Babble-On.

 

[Hayden]

So, as Kai has been babbling on for god knows how long, I thought I’d take over. I’m Hayden and I’m in charge of making sure the show actually works technically!  We’ve been very lucky during the lockdown as we’ve been able to keep our station on air with remote broadcasting (This next bit is for the radio tech nerds). We have a fully digital desk in our studio which allows us to control the faders remotely. We then connect to one of our PC’s in the studio using Anydesk and set up our Cleanfeed connection. 

 

Cleanfeed is an awesome tool that we’ve been using throughout the lockdown that allows us to send and receive high-quality audio through the internet. It’s completely free too (with a paid pro version for more features). With the fader turned up for the PC and a mix-minus we’re able to broadcast remotely. All we have to do is mute and unmute our mics! 

 

As for the phone-in side of things we reached out to Broadcast Bionics about their new software called CallerOne. They were very kind and gave us a free 3-month license to use during our show, so big thanks to them. We’ve also purchased a SIP (session initiation protocol) number which is basically an online phone number. From there we just needed to insert the login details into CallerOne and voila! CallerOne is accessible remotely via our VPN which lets our call-screeners (Ellie, James and Molly!) screen calls beforehand and then put them through to us to be brought on air.

 

While planning the project we thought, how can we expand our reach and gain a wider audience? We decided that we’d stream the show live on Facebook and Twitter, as song copyrights wouldn’t be an issue. We use a piece of software that a lot of you will be familiar with called Open Broadcaster Software (OBS) which allows us to stream the webstream on any platform we choose. We use that in conjunction with a website called restream.io which allows us to broadcast to more than one place at a time. This is how we caught the attention of Iain Lee as he’d seen out stream on Twitter!

 

 

[Kai]

 

The reaction we’ve had to the show so far has been incredible. In little under 3 weeks, it has gained a really nice following of callers who have each embraced what the show is aimed to be, an escape in the early hours. From discussions of mental health in lockdown to the right way to make a cup of tea, we’ve had calls from people as far as Cardiff & Cyprus, we may focus on issues local to Sunderland, but it seems like any community can relate, especially right now. 

 

We do throw out topics, but we love it when our listeners guide the conversation because that’s when it comes to life & truly feels special. We aimed our show at the radio listener who is looking for company late at night, but we’ve also discovered a second, quite unexpected audience, that being the ‘radio geek’, who loves this genre, is interested in how we’re doing it, and finds the show nostalgic of radio gone by. As self-proclaimed radio geeks ourselves, we’ve loved this too. 

 

Like most call-in shows, we do have sneaky back-up callers ready to go if we have a quiet night, but we’ve been overwhelmed by the response to the number of callers we’ve received so far. The feeling of seeing a new caller number from a stranger popping up on the board never gets old, especially when it then leads to incredible conversations, such as the surprise one we had with an Iain from Aylesbury.

 

The radio industry is changing, late-night talk radio in the commercial world may never be the same again, so this is the moment that student radio can step up, fill the gaps & make our mark. 

 

We’d love for you to be a part of the show. Give us a call on 0191 359 2459, Mondays to Thursdays on Spark.
